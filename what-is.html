What is a starter?

Essentially, a starter is a mixture of flour, water, and a bit of patience (aka bacteria from the air). It is how we cultivate wild yeast in a form that we can use.
Wild yeast is present in all flour and the easiest way to access it is by combining the flour and water and letting it sit for days.

After a couple days, bubbles will start to form in your starter. At this moment the yeast is starting to become active and is multiplying, getting stronger.
To maintain the pleasant activity coming from the yeast, we feed the starter fresh flour and water until it becomes frothy and begins to increase in size. Only then is it ready to be used.

Sourdough Starter Recipe

  • Time: 5 minutes
  • Serves: Infinitely, as long as you keep it alive

Equiptment

  • kitchen scale
  • 2-quart glass/plastic container (no metal)
  • clean hand or mixing spoon
  • Clean kitchen towel
  • Rubber band

Ingredients

  • 4 ounces all-purpose flour (63 grams)
  • 4 ounces water, room temp (63 grams)

Directions

  1. Place containter on the kitchen scale and tare it out to begin measuring ingredients.
  2. First begin by measuring out the water, then tare it out.
  3. To the water, add the flour.
  4. Combine then together, ideally with your clean hand to aid in adding natural bacteria, but if grosses you out, feel free to use a mixing utensil .
  5. Scrape down the sides and loosely cover the container with the clean towel, secure with rubber band.
  6. Place the container somewhere with consistent room temp (70-75 °F).
  7. Check on it after 24 hours!
  8. Repeat these steps by adding the same ratio of flour/water daily, for 4 days or until ripe enough for use.
